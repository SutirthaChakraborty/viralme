FAQ: What is the difference between animation and motion graphics?

\n
What is animation?
\n
Animation is the process of creating moving images or videos using various techniques such as keyframes, transitions, and transforms. It can be used to add life and movement to static images or to create complex visual effects in video content.
\n
What is motion graphics?
\n
Motion graphics are a type of animation that use text and graphic elements to create moving visuals. They often involve the use of text animations, which can include things like typing, handwriting, and speech bubbles. Motion graphics can be used for a variety of purposes, including advertising, explainer videos, and social media content.
