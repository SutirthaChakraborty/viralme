Best Time-lapse Interval for Construction

Choosing the Right Interval

When creating a time-lapse of a construction project, the best interval depends on the speed of the construction and the desired smoothness of the video. According to experts, a capture interval of 5 minutes to 15 minutes is recommended for construction projects, but the ideal interval can vary based on the specific project and the desired outcome.

Factors to Consider

Consider the pace of the construction, the amount of change occurring between each capture, and the desired length of the final time-lapse video. It's important to find a balance between capturing enough frames for a meaningful compilation and avoiding unnecessary extra frames.

Technical Considerations

When setting up the time-lapse interval, ensure that the intervalometer settings are configured correctly and that the system can maintain these settings without interruption. Additionally, consider the storage requirements for the captured frames and the ease of transferring and processing the images.
