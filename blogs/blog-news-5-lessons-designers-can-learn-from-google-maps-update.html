5 Lessons Designers Can Learn from Google Maps Update

Google Maps has recently undergone a significant update, offering valuable insights for designers looking to enhance their projects. Here are five lessons that can be drawn from this update:

  • Simplicity and Intuitiveness: Google Maps has streamlined its interface, making it easier for users to navigate. Designers can learn from this by focusing on simplicity and ensuring their designs are intuitive to users.
  • Empowerment of Users: Google Maps allows users to contribute to the map's accuracy, showcasing the importance of empowering users in design. Designers should consider how users can interact with their designs and contribute to their content.
  • Contextual Relevance: The update emphasizes relevant and contextual information, highlighting the need for designers to focus on providing useful and timely information to users.
  • Feedback and Iteration: Google Maps continuously gathers feedback and iterates on its design, demonstrating the importance of user feedback in the design process. Designers should be open to feedback and willing to iterate on their designs.
  • Accessibility: The update has improved accessibility features, showing the importance of designing with accessibility in mind. Designers should strive to create accessible designs that can be used by everyone.
