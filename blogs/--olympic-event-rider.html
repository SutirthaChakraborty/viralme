Olympic Event Rider

Experience the thrill of Olympic Event Riding, a demanding equestrian discipline that tests athletes in three phases: Dressage, Cross-Country, and Show Jumping. Each phase showcases the rider's skill, agility, and partnership with their horse.

Key Phases of Olympic Eventing

  • Dressage
  • Cross-Country
  • Show Jumping

Dressage

This phase judges the horse's training and the rider's ability to communicate with their horse. It includes a series of predetermined movements performed in an arena.

Cross-Country

In this challenging phase, riders navigate a course of natural obstacles over varied terrain, testing endurance and bravery.

Show Jumping

The final phase requires precision and agility as horse and rider navigate a course of jumps, with penalties for knocked rails.
