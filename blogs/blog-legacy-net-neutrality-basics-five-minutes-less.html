Understanding Net Neutrality in 5 Minutes

What is Net Neutrality?

Net neutrality is the principle that internet service providers (ISPs) must treat all data on the internet the same. This means no throttling, blocking, or prioritizing of content.

Why is it Important?

Net neutrality is crucial for a free and open internet, allowing equal access to information and services, promoting innovation, and supporting small businesses and startups.

Key Points About Net Neutrality:

  • Prevents ISPs from controlling what you see online.
  • Ensures equal treatment of all content.
  • Encourages free speech and competition.
  • Affects various sectors, from education to healthcare.
