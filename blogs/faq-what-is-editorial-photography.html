Frequently Asked Questions

What is Editorial Photography?

Editorial photography is a genre of photography that aims to illustrate a story or concept within the context of media publications such as magazines, newspapers, or online platforms. Unlike commercial photography, which focuses on selling products, editorial images provide a narrative about a topic.

What is the purpose of Editorial Photography?

The purpose of editorial photography is to enhance storytelling. It accompanies articles, interviews, and features, providing a visual representation of the content and engaging the audience on an emotional level.

How does it differ from Commercial Photography?

While commercial photography focuses on marketing and sales, editorial photography is about storytelling and conveying messages. Editorial images may not directly promote a product but instead offer context or provoke thought about specific subjects.
