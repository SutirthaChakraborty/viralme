Video Resolutions & Aspect Ratios

What is Video Resolution?

Video resolution refers to the amount of pixels displayed in each dimension on a screen. Common resolutions include 720p, 1080p, 4K, and 8K. Higher resolution means more detail and clarity.

Resolution affects video quality and is crucial for viewing on larger screens. Common aspect ratios include 16:9 for HD and 4:3 for standard definition.

Understanding Aspect Ratios

Aspect ratio is the ratio of a video's width to its height. Common aspect ratios include 16:9, 4:3, and 21:9, which significantly influence how content is perceived on various devices.

Choosing the appropriate aspect ratio is essential for creating an immersive viewing experience, particularly for films and games.
