Email Marketing: The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly

The Good

Using **web-safe fonts** like Arial and Verdana ensures your emails look consistent across devices. **Responsive design** enhances user experience, making your emails more engaging.

The Bad

Overly decorative fonts can lead to **readability issues**. Avoid using too many different fonts in one email, as it can create a cluttered look.

The Ugly

Using **non-standard fonts** can result in fallback issues, leading to a poor user experience. Always provide a **font stack** for better compatibility.

1. Keep your design simple and clean.
2. Use clear and concise subject lines.
3. Optimize for mobile devices.
4. Test your emails before sending.
