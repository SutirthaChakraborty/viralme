European Commission Portfolio

Overview

The European Commission is the executive branch of the European Union, responsible for proposing legislation, implementing decisions, upholding the EU treaties, and managing the day-to-day business of the EU.

Key Functions

**Policy Development:** The Commission develops EU policies and initiatives across various sectors, including trade, environment, and competition.

**Legislative Proposal:** It has the sole right to initiate EU legislation, which is then reviewed and adopted by the European Parliament and the Council of the European Union.

**Enforcement:** The Commission ensures that EU laws are correctly applied and implemented in member states.

Role in the EU

The Commission plays a crucial role in shaping the EU's strategic priorities and represents the EU on the international stage in areas such as trade negotiations and climate action.
