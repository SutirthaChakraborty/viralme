European Broadcasting Union

The European Broadcasting Union (EBU) is an alliance of public service media organizations from the European Broadcasting Area and the Council of Europe. It consists of 123 member organizations from 56 countries and 31 associate members from 20 countries.

For more details, click here.

The EBU was created in 1950 to address the need for a strong organization to coordinate broadcasting activities in western Europe. It operates almost 2,000 television, radio, and online channels and services, reaching audiences of more than one billion people worldwide in over 160 languages.
