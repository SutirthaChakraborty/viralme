Tips for Producing High-Quality Video with Limited Resources

1. Plan Your Shots
Creating a storyboard can help maximize your shooting efficiency.
2. Use Natural Light
Maximize available daylight by shooting near windows without artificial light.
3. Invest in Good Audio
Quality audio is crucial; consider cheaper external mics instead of built-in ones.
4. Keep it Steady
Use a tripod, or stabilize your camera using household objects to avoid shaky footage.
5. Edit Wisely
Use free or low-cost editing software to enhance your video without breaking the bank.
