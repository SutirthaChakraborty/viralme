COVID-19

What is COVID-19?

COVID-19 is a contagious disease caused by a new strain of coronavirus. It was first identified in Wuhan, China in December 2019.

COVID-19 is spread from person to person through respiratory droplets that are produced when an infected person talks, coughs or sneezes.

The symptoms of COVID-19 include fever, cough, shortness of breath, and fatigue.

There is no specific treatment for COVID-19, but most people recover from the illness without needing hospitalization.

How is COVID-19 spread?

COVID-19 is spread through respiratory droplets that are produced when an infected person talks, coughs or sneezes.

The droplets can land on surfaces and objects, and then be picked up by another person who touches the surface or object.

COVID-19 can also be spread through close contact with an infected person, such as shaking hands or sharing food and drinks.

Wearing a mask, washing your hands frequently, and avoiding close contact with others are ways to reduce the spread of COVID-19.

What can you do to prevent COVID-19?

The best way to prevent COVID-19 is to avoid close contact with others who are sick, and to wear a mask when you are in public.

Washing your hands frequently with soap and water is also important.

Avoiding touching your face, especially your mouth, nose, and eyes, can also help prevent the spread of COVID-19.

Staying home from work or school if you are sick, and avoiding sharing food and drinks with others, can also help prevent the spread of COVID-19.

What are the symptoms of COVID-19?

The symptoms of COVID-19 include:

  • Fever
  • Cough
  • Shortness of breath
  • Fatigue

If you are experiencing any of these symptoms, you should seek medical attention.

What is the treatment for COVID-19?

There is no specific treatment for COVID-19, but most people recover from the illness without needing hospitalization.

Rest, hydration, and over-the-counter medications such as acetaminophen or ibuprofen can help alleviate symptoms.

Antiviral medications may also be prescribed in severe cases of COVID-19.

How can you get tested for COVID-19?

If you are experiencing symptoms of COVID-19, you should contact your healthcare provider to see if you need to be tested.

Testing for COVID-19 typically involves a swab of the nose or throat, and the results are usually available within a few days.
