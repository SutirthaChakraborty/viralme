Customs House Overview

What is a Customs House?

A Customs House is a government building where customs duties are collected and goods are cleared for import/export.

Customs Houses play a crucial role in international trade, ensuring compliance with local laws and regulations. They facilitate the flow of goods while preventing smuggling and ensuring safety standards.

Functions of a Customs House include:

  • Collection of duties and taxes on imported goods
  • Inspection and verification of cargo
  • Enforcement of trade regulations
