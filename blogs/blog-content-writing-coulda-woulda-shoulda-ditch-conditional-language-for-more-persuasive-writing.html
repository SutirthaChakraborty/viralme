Coulda, Woulda, Shoulda: Ditch Conditional Language for More Persuasive Writing

Effective writing inspires action. To enhance your persuasive writing, eliminate conditional phrases like "coulda," "woulda," and "shoulda." These terms weaken your message and create doubt.

Instead, use assertive language that encourages readers to take action. For example, replace "You could try our product" with "Try our product today!" This shift in language can significantly impact your audience's response.
