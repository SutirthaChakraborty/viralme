Mary Elmes Bridge

Explore the architectural marvel and community connector in Cork, Ireland

About the Bridge

Mary Elmes Bridge, originally known for its design by architect Sir William Atkins, stands as a tribute to the humanitarian efforts of Mary Elmes. This iconic bridge not only connects different parts of Cork but also symbolizes support and unity in the community.

Historical Significance

Mary Elmes was a courageous aid worker during World War II. The bridge named after her serves as a reminder of her commitment to bridging divides and fostering human connection.

Architectural Features

Designed with modern sustainability principles, the bridge features pedestrian walkways and scenic views of the River Lee, making it a popular spot for locals and tourists alike.
