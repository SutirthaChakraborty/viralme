About the River Liffey

The **River Liffey** is a river in Ireland that flows through the center of Dublin. It is approximately 125 kilometers (78 miles) long and divides the city into the Northside and Southside. The name Liffey comes from the Irish word "An Life," which means "the Liffey."

Significance

The River Liffey has significant historical, economic, and cultural importance to the city of Dublin. It has been a vital source of water and transportation for centuries and is a central feature of the city's landscape.
