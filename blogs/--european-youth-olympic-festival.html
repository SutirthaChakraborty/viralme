European Youth Olympic Festival

What is the European Youth Olympic Festival?

The European Youth Olympic Festival (EYOF) is a multi-sport event for young athletes aged 14 to 18 from across Europe. It promotes the Olympic spirit and encourages sports among the youth.

This festival is organized every two years and serves as a stepping stone for young athletes aiming for future Olympic participation.

History of EYOF

The inaugural EYOF took place in 1991, with participants from 36 countries. Since then, it has grown, both in terms of participants and the number of sports included.

The festival has been held in various cities, and every edition inspires future generations of athletes and promotes unity among European youth.

Participating Sports

EYOF features a wide array of sports including athletics, swimming, gymnastics, and winter sports like skiing and ice hockey, depending on the season of the festival.

This variety allows young athletes to showcase their skills and represents the diversity of sports across Europe.
