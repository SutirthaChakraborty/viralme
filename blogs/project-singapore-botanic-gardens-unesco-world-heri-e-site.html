Singapore Botanic Gardens

A UNESCO World Heritage Site

Overview

The Singapore Botanic Gardens is a tropical garden located in Singapore's Orchard Road district, renowned for its extensive collection of flora, including a world-famous orchid garden.

Designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2015, it is a testament to the historical significance and biodiversity of the region.

Explore More

The gardens play a crucial role in conservation, botanical research, and education about plant biodiversity in a rapidly urbanizing environment.

They host numerous programs aimed at increasing awareness about sustainability and eco-friendly practices.
