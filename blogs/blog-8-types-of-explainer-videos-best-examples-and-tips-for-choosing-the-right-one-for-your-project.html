Introduction to Explainer Videos

Explainer videos are a powerful tool for businesses and organizations to explain complex ideas, products, or services in a concise and engaging way. They are designed to communicate information quickly and effectively, making them ideal for both online and offline use.

Types of Explainer Videos

There are several types of explainer videos, each serving a unique purpose:

  • Whiteboard Animation
  • 2D Character Animation
  • 3D Animation
  • Motion Graphics
  • Stop Motion Animation
  • Live Action
  • Slide Animation
  • Interactive Video

Best Examples of Explainer Videos

Here are some of the best examples of explainer videos across different types:

  • Whiteboard Animation: This is Water by David Foster Wallace
  • 2D Character Animation: The Life of Pi by Wieden+Kennedy
  • 3D Animation: Toyota's Empty Chair by Dentsu
  • Motion Graphics: The Story of Light Bulbs by The Economist
  • Stop Motion Animation: The Nut Job by Rainmaker Entertainment
  • Live Action: Gap's Change is Good by Wieden+Kennedy
  • Slide Animation: The Life Cycle of a Virus by TED-Ed
  • Interactive Video: A History of the World in 100 Objects by the BBC

Choosing the Right Explainer Video

Selecting the right explainer video depends on your audience, the complexity of your subject, and your goals. Consider the following tips:

  • Understand your audience and their preferences.
  • Choose a style that complements your brand identity.
  • Keep the message clear and concise.
  • Incorporate visuals and animations that enhance understanding.
  • A/B test different videos to see what resonates best with your audience.
