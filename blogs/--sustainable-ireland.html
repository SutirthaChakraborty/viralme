Sustainable Ireland

What is Sustainability?

Sustainability refers to meeting our own needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. It encompasses environmental, economic, and social dimensions.

Ireland is investing heavily in renewable energy sources like wind, solar, and biomass. The aim is to reduce carbon emissions and dependence on fossil fuels.

Irish farmers are adopting sustainable farming practices such as crop rotation, organic farming, and agroforestry to enhance biodiversity and soil health.

Efforts in Ireland include protecting natural habitats, restoring ecosystems, and conserving native species to maintain biodiversity.

Promoting public transport, cycling, and electric vehicles are key strategies in reducing emissions and building a sustainable transport system.

Join the Movement

Everyone can contribute to a more sustainable Ireland. Small actions like reducing waste, conserving water, and supporting local businesses can make a significant impact.
