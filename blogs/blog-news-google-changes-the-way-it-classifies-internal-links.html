Google recently announced changes to the way it classifies internal links within its search results. This update is part of the company's ongoing efforts to improve the user experience and the relevance of search results.

What are internal links?

Internal links are hyperlinks that direct users from one page on a website to another page on the same website. They are an essential part of website architecture, helping to organize content and improve navigation.

Why the change?

Google's decision to update its approach to internal link classification is driven by a desire to provide more accurate and useful search results. By better understanding the structure of websites, Google aims to deliver more relevant information to users.

Impact on webmasters and SEOs

For webmasters and SEO professionals, this change signals the importance of creating a well-structured website. Proper use of internal links can now play a significant role in improving a site's ranking in search engine results.
