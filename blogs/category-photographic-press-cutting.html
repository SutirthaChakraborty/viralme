Photographic Press Cutting

What is Photographic Press Cutting?
Photographic press cutting refers to the collection of published articles and images from newspapers and magazines. This process captures visuals in a physical format, often used for historical records, research, and media analysis.
Importance of Press Cuttings
Press cuttings serve as valuable resources for journalists, researchers, and businesses to track media coverage, understand public perception, and evaluate the impact of press releases.
How to Compile Photographic Press Cuttings
Compiling involves sourcing articles, scanning images, and organizing them chronologically or thematically. Digital tools can enhance this process, making it efficient for modern use.
