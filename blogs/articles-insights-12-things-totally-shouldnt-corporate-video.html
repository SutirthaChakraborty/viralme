Corporate Video: 12 Things You Totally Shouldn't Do

1. Avoid Overly Salesy Tone

Your video should focus on telling a story, not just pushing products.

2. Don't Forget the Call to Action

Make sure your viewers know what you want them to do next.

3. Keep It Short and Sweet

People have short attention spans, so keep your video to under 2 minutes.

4. Don't Ignore the Script

Even if you're a great speaker, a good script helps convey your message clearly.

5. Don't Underestimate the Power of Visuals

Use high-quality images and videos to engage your audience.

6. Don't Forget to Show the Human Side

Share stories of success, challenges, and the people behind the brand.

7. Don't Overuse Music

Music can be distracting and overpowering; use it sparingly.

8. Don't Forget to Edit Out Flubs

No one is perfect; cut out any mistakes you make.

9. Don't Rely on Text-Heavy Slides

Use slides sparingly and make sure they enhance the video, not detract from it.

10. Don't Forget to Showcase Your Unique Selling Proposition

Highlight what makes your brand different and why people should care.

11. Don't Use Too Many Effects

Effects can be distracting and detract from the message. Keep it simple.

12. Don't Forget to Test and Refine

Show your video to a focus group or trusted colleagues for feedback.
