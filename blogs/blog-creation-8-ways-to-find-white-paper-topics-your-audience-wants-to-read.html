8 Ways to Find White Paper Topics Your Audience Wants

1. Audience Surveys

Engage your audience with surveys to gather insights on their interests.

Utilize tools like Google Forms or SurveyMonkey to create an anonymous survey.

2. Social Media Trends

Monitor social media platforms for trending topics your audience is discussing.

Use tools like BuzzSumo or Twitter trending topics to find relevant subjects.

3. Industry Forums

Participate in online forums to discover what topics are being asked about.

Explore sites like Reddit or Quora for popular discussions in your niche.

4. Competitor Analysis

Analyze competitors' white papers for inspiration and potential gaps.

Look at competitorsâ€™ websites or use tools like SEMrush for insights.

5. Google Keyword Planner

Use Google Keyword Planner to identify keywords your audience is searching for.

This tool helps you understand the search volume and competition for various topics.

6. Customer Feedback

Review customer feedback and inquiries for ideas on relevant topics.

Implement a feedback mechanism on your website or during customer interactions.

7. Content Gaps

Identify content gaps in your existing materials for new white paper topics.

Review analytics to see which topics your audience engages with the least.

8. Expert Interviews

Interview industry experts to uncover what topics they believe are critical.

Use these insights to create deep, informative white papers that resonate with your audience.
