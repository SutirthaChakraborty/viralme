Video Content Virality & Egocentricity

Insights from a Harvard Professor

Recent discussions led by a Harvard professor emphasize how video content virality is significantly influenced by egocentricity. This phenomenon explains why certain videos resonate more with audiences, primarily when they serve personal interests or celebrate individual achievements.

When videos connect with viewers on a personal level, they create a stronger emotional response, driving shares and engagement. Understanding this intricate relationship between content and viewer psychology is critical for content creators aiming for virality.
